# Revenue Leverage — Third-Party Angles

**Date:** 2026-04-23
**Context:** BC's question — where else do we have leverage to get paid by someone other than the salons? The platform already drives material activity through third-party services. Listing the angles ranked by realistic near-term value.

---

## Tier 1 — Real money, reachable in the near term

### 1. Stripe volume rebate
**Angle:** Stripe's LATAM enterprise team offers volume discounts + rebates to processors handling ≥$X/month. We're building to eight-figure MXN/year on card processing. Ask for: (a) reduced processing rate (standard 3.6% + $3 MXN → negotiate 3.4% or flat $2 MXN), (b) monthly rebate on processed volume, (c) Connect-specific perks (faster onboarding, better dispute ratios).
**Effort:** One email to a Stripe LATAM enterprise rep. They'll respond.
**Timing:** Start now. The sooner we're on their enterprise track, the better the rate when volume lands.

### 2. Uber + DiDi (already drafted)
**Angle:** Referral commission on round-trip rides we schedule. Letters drafted in this folder.
**Effort:** Send letters + follow-up cadence.
**Timing:** Now.

### 3. Product supplier rebates (POS)
**Angle:** L'Oreal, Wella, Revlon, Schwarzkopf, Maybelline — these brands pay for retail shelf placement in physical stores. Our POS is digital shelf space with built-in conversion tracking, demographic data, and geographic coverage across Mexico. Pitch: featured-brand slots, category sponsorship, data-sharing partnerships (aggregated, anonymized).
**Effort:** Medium — need meaningful salon count first (say, 100+ active POS-enabled salons) to pitch credibly. Foundation work now, pitch in 3-6 months.
**Timing:** Seed relationships now, serious pitch when we have volume.

---

## Tier 2 — Real but needs more volume / product work first

### 4. Meta Ads / TikTok Ads reseller program
**Angle:** When salons start paying for marketing to drive clients to their BeautyCita page, they'll want Meta and TikTok ads. Become an authorized partner / reseller and take a cut. Meta's Business Partner Program is formalized; TikTok has similar.
**Effort:** Medium-high — partner application process, may require minimum spend thresholds.
**Timing:** Needs our own salon marketing product built first (Q3 2026 probably).

### 5. WhatsApp / Infobip volume rebates
**Angle:** We're migrating from whatsapp-web.js to Infobip as our WhatsApp Business API provider. Infobip has enterprise volume tiers. Negotiate rebates when our message volume scales (conservatively, 100K+ messages/month once salons are using automated marketing triggers).
**Effort:** Low — one email once we're ready to commit to Infobip.
**Timing:** Start conversation now to get best rate from day one.

### 6. Salon equipment / furniture marketplace
**Angle:** Salons need chairs, stations, mirrors, sinks. List preferred suppliers in the business panel. Take a referral fee on every furniture order. Brands like Salon Ambience, Belvedere, Collins.
**Effort:** Medium — needs a supplier partnership and a merchandising surface in the business panel.
**Timing:** 6-month horizon. Useful add-on once salon panel maturity is higher.

### 7. POS hardware terminal resale
**Angle:** Salons that enable POS need a card terminal. Clip, SumUp, Mercado Pago all have reseller / affiliate programs in Mexico. We funnel the order + take a kickback.
**Effort:** Low — sign up to their affiliate programs, add a "Get a terminal" CTA in the POS setup flow.
**Timing:** Can ship in a week once POS activations are happening.

---

## Tier 3 — Interesting, not urgent

### 8. Insurance — stylist liability + gear
**Angle:** Independent stylists are uninsured. Professional liability insurance is a real market. Partner with an insurer (GNP, AXA, Qualitas) for a white-label or affiliate offer. Take a commission on each policy sold through BC.
**Effort:** High — insurance partnerships are slow and regulated.
**Timing:** 12+ months.

### 9. Banking affinity (non-loan)
**Angle:** Small-business accounts, debit cards, payroll services for salon owners. Banks pay referral fees for account openings (~$500-2,000 MXN per opened account, variable by product). Santander, Banorte, HSBC, BBVA all have affinity partnership programs.
**Effort:** High.
**Timing:** Aligns with our own banking setup timeline.
**Note:** Keep this fully separate from the private loan program concept (per memory — loan program is internal only, never in external-facing materials).

### 10. Beauty school / academy partnerships
**Angle:** Schools pay to have their graduates featured to salons with job openings. Or: academy-certified stylists get a badge on BC. Academy pays per placement. Beauty schools like Jafra, Jean Luis David Academy, CECAE.
**Effort:** Medium.
**Timing:** Needs the stylist-ownership side of the platform more mature.

### 11. Photo printing / business card partnerships
**Angle:** Low-margin volume. Probably not worth the chase unless a clean integration exists.
**Skip unless someone brings it to us.**

### 12. Aesthetic tourism partnerships (hotels)
**Angle:** Puerto Vallarta, Cabo, Cancun — beauty tourism is real. Hotels could cross-promote with us. Too bespoke to systematize at current scale.
**Skip for now.**

---

## What NOT to pursue

- **Advertising revenue from salons themselves.** This violates the "never charge salons" philosophy. Not a leverage angle; a cannibalization risk.
- **Data licensing.** Selling user data (even aggregated) opens LFPDPPP exposure and pisses off users if discovered. Hard pass unless there's a dedicated consent flow and BC is OK with the brand risk.
- **Featured-salon pay-to-play ranking.** Same problem — compromises the "intelligent agent" neutrality. Bright line.

## Why these matter

Current revenue model is all salon-side (3% commission). Every angle above is a revenue stream that doesn't increase salon cost, keeping the "free for salon clients, cheap for BC bookings" promise intact while diversifying BC's income. Stripe + Uber + product rebates alone could plausibly match or exceed salon-commission revenue by year 3 if volume materializes.
