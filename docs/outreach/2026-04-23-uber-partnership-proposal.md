Dear Uber Partnerships Team,

I write on behalf of BeautyCita, S.A. de C.V., a Mexican corporation operating the country's first intelligent booking agent for beauty services (beautycita.com — iOS, Android, and web). We have indexed more than 76,000 beauty establishments across Mexico and are actively onboarding salons in Mexico City, Guadalajara, Monterrey, Puerto Vallarta, Cancun, and Tijuana.

Every booking on our platform triggers a transportation decision at checkout. Alongside personal vehicle and public transit, Uber is offered as the premium option. When a client selects Uber, we schedule a **round-trip** — an outbound ride at appointment time and a return ride after the service is completed — using your public deep-link API.

I am writing to request a conversation about a formal referral partnership. At our current onboarding velocity and conservative conversion assumptions, we project more than **10,000 round-trip Uber rides per month** originating from BeautyCita within the next twelve months. Each of those rides is:

- A scheduled pickup handed to Uber with full metadata (origin, destination, appointment time, rider identity)
- A round-trip — doubling the realized value per referral compared to typical one-way demand
- Anchored to a firm clock — beauty appointments are time-committed, reducing the no-show rate relative to casual-hail demand
- A rider we introduced, qualified, and actively directed to your service

We would like to discuss three subjects:

1. **Per-ride referral economics.** A revenue share or per-ride fee on rides originated from BeautyCita, tracked via a partner identifier, promo code, or dedicated attribution link.
2. **Integration upgrades beyond the public deep-link API.** Specifically, scheduled-ride scheduling, arrival-time estimates, and reschedule synchronization. These capabilities are currently absent from the public integration and would materially improve the client experience while increasing the volume we steer toward your service.
3. **Co-marketing.** Our demographic — majority women, 18 to 45, in urban and mid-market Mexico, with recurring discretionary spend on personal care — is a defined, valuable audience. We believe it overlaps strongly with yours, and cross-promotion could benefit both platforms.

4. **Exclusivity.** As part of a finalized agreement, BeautyCita is prepared to offer exclusive ride-hailing partnership on our platform. That means Uber would be the sole ride-hailing option promoted to our users, with competing services removed from the transport-selection interface at checkout and from any in-app transport recommendation we deliver. This exclusivity is on the table as part of the negotiation; we believe it materially increases the value we can deliver to a single committed partner.

BeautyCita is investing in the integration, the infrastructure, and the user education required to make Uber the default transport option across our platform. We believe the scale we are building warrants a partnership conversation rather than a standard public-API relationship.

I welcome a thirty-minute call with your LATAM partnerships team at your convenience. I am happy to provide traffic projections by region, technical documentation of our current integration, corporate documentation of BeautyCita, S.A. de C.V., and any other information required to evaluate the opportunity.

Thank you for your time and consideration.
