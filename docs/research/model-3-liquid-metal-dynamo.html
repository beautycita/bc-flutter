Liquid Metal Vortex Dynamo

What Is This?

Deep inside Earth, a river of molten iron flows in complex spirals. That flowing conductor generates our planet's magnetic field through a process called the dynamo effect: moving conductive fluid creates electric currents, which create magnetic fields, which push the fluid, amplifying the whole cycle.

This model simulates the same physics at desktop scale. A liquid metal alloy (GaInSn — gallium-indium-tin) flows through a toroidal chamber. Above a critical flow speed, the system spontaneously generates its own magnetic field — dynamo ignition.

Ancient texts describe mercury placed inside sealed chambers within pyramids. Whatever the original purpose, the physics of flowing liquid metal generating magnetic fields is real and reproducible. This is that experiment.

The Science

Dynamo Theory: A conducting fluid moving fast enough through a magnetic field induces currents that regenerate and amplify that field. The key dimensionless number is the Magnetic Reynolds Number:

Rm = mu_0 * sigma * v * L

Where mu_0 = 4pi x 10^-7 H/m (permeability of free space), sigma = electrical conductivity (S/m), v = flow velocity (m/s), L = characteristic length (m).

Critical Threshold: When Rm exceeds ~10-100 (depends on geometry; ~40 for an optimized toroidal vortex), the feedback loop becomes self-sustaining. Below this, any seed field decays. Above it, the field grows exponentially until saturated by back-reaction.

The Feedback Loop:

  • Flowing metal cuts through ambient field lines
  • This induces electric currents (Faraday's law)
  • Currents produce their own magnetic field (Ampere's law)
  • The new field is carried and stretched by the flow
  • If Rm > critical: field grows. If not: field decays.

Liquid Metal Comparison:

  • Mercury: sigma = 1.04 MS/m. Classic choice but toxic. Needs very high flow speeds.
  • GaInSn: sigma = 3.46 MS/m. Non-toxic, liquid at room temp. 3x better than mercury. The practical choice.
  • Sodium: sigma = 21 MS/m. Best conductor but explosive in air/water. Used in Riga and Karlsruhe experiments.

Saturated Field Estimate:

B ≈ sqrt(mu_0 * rho * v^2) * (1 - Rm_c/Rm)

Once ignited, field strength scales with kinetic energy density of the flow, modulated by how far above critical you are.

Connection to Earth: Earth's core has Rm ~ 200-1000. Liquid iron at ~5000 K, flowing at ~0.5 mm/s over thousands of km. Same physics, vastly different scale.

The Pyramid Mercury Hypothesis: Multiple ancient sources describe mercury in sealed chambers within pyramids. If mercury was made to flow (via thermal convection from geothermal heat or solar heating), it would — given sufficient geometry and speed — create a dynamo. Whether the ancients understood this physics is debated. That the physics works is not.

How To Build It

Bill of Materials:

  • Toroidal chamber: 316L stainless steel or borosilicate glass, ID 2-4cm, OD 30-50cm. Custom glassblowing or CNC machined halves with O-ring seal.
  • GaInSn alloy: ~500mL-2L depending on torus size. ~$500-2000/kg from Indium Corporation or RotoMetals.
  • Electromagnetic pump: No moving parts in contact with metal. Lorentz-force pump using external magnets + current. Or: mechanical impeller pump with ceramic bearings.
  • Gaussmeter: 3-axis Hall probe, 0.01 Gauss resolution minimum. Lakeshore 475 DSP (lab grade) or AlphaLab GM2 (hobby).
  • Pickup coils: 500-1000 turn copper coils, ~2cm diameter, placed at 3-4 positions around torus.
  • Data acquisition: Arduino + ADS1115 ADC for coil voltage, or NI DAQ for lab precision.
  • Helmholtz seed coils: Optional — provides initial field to measure Rm indirectly even below critical.
  • Flow meter: Ultrasonic Doppler (liquid metals are opaque).

Estimated cost: $3,000 - $15,000 depending on precision level.

Safety: GaInSn is non-toxic but stains everything. Gallium attacks aluminum — do not use aluminum parts. Keep below 100C (melting point of indium-tin phases).

What To Measure

Gaussmeter Placement:

  • Position 1: On the axis of the torus (center of the ring) — strongest dipolar component
  • Position 2: Outside the torus, equatorial plane — quadrupolar component
  • Position 3: Above/below the torus — dipolar vertical component

What Constitutes Dynamo Ignition:

  • Apply a brief seed field with Helmholtz coils, then turn them off
  • Below critical Rm: field decays exponentially back to zero
  • At critical Rm: field persists (neither grows nor decays)
  • Above critical Rm: field grows exponentially, then saturates
  • The smoking gun: turn off the pump — field persists briefly then decays with magnetic diffusion timescale, NOT the pump-off timescale

Expected Field Ramp-Up:

B(t) = B_seed * exp(gamma * t)
gamma = (Rm/Rm_c - 1) / tau_diff
tau_diff = mu_0 * sigma * L^2

For GaInSn in a 40cm torus: tau_diff ≈ 70 ms. Growth rate at Rm = 2*Rm_c: gamma ≈ 14/s. Field doubles every ~50ms. Saturation in ~0.5 seconds.
